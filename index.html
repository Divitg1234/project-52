Interesting Facts About The Camel

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Interesting Facts About The Panda

  • They have great camouflage for their environment.
  • Their eyes are different to normal bears.
  • Bamboo is critical to their diet.

Interesting Facts About The cicada

  • Cicadas Live on All Continents Except Antarctica.
  • They Have One of the Longest Insect Life Spans.
  • They Only Emerge When the Ground Is 64 Degrees.

Interesting Facts About The chameleon

  • Skin Crystals Enable Them to Change Colour at Will
  • Their Feet Work Like Salad Tongs
  • Almost Half of All Known Species Live in Madagascar

Interesting Facts About The pigeon

  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.

Interesting Facts About The teddy

  • The incident of Roosevelt sparing the bear led to the popularity and naming of Teddy bears.
  • The story of Goldilocks And The Three Bears was written in 1837 by Robert Southey who was the Poet Laureate at the time.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
